What is Bcrypt?

Bcrypt is a password-hashing function designed by Niels Provos and David Mazieres, based on the Blowfish cipher. It incorporates a cost factor (also called work factor), which determines how much computational effort is required to compute the hash. This makes it resistant to brute-force attacks even as computing power increases.

Why Use Bcrypt?

  • Adaptive Hashing: The cost factor can be increased over time to keep pace with advances in hardware
  • Slow by Design: Intentionally computationally expensive to slow down brute-force attacks
  • Built-in Salt: Each hash includes a unique salt to prevent rainbow table attacks
  • Battle-Tested: Widely used and recommended for password hashing since 1999

Cost Factor Explained

The cost factor is exponential. Each increment doubles the computation time:

Cost Factor Iterations Approx. Time
10 (default) 1,024 ~100ms
11 2,048 ~200ms
12 4,096 ~400ms
13 8,192 ~800ms

Recommendation: Use a cost factor of 10-12 for most applications. Higher values provide better security but slower performance.
